# Mission 09: Behavioral Frameworks & Theory (2020-2026)

## Context

Config-driven RL for JITAIs. PEARL uses the COM-B framework. This scan maps the landscape of behavioral frameworks applied to digital health interventions, identifying alternatives, extensions, cultural adaptations, digital-specific modifications, and documented failures.

## Key Findings

### 1. Framework Prevalence in Digital Health (2020-2026)

| Framework | Prevalence | Trend |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| COM-B / BCW | High | **Rapidly growing** since 2022 |
| TDF | Medium-High | Increasing use alongside COM-B |
| TPB | High | **Stable** -- most common in cross-sectional studies |
| SCT | High | Stable, especially for physical activity |
| HBM | Medium | Declining relative to COM-B |
| BCTTv1 | Medium | Common for auditing, less for design |
| JITAI | Low-Medium | Emerging, limited theory integration |

### 2. When Frameworks Fail

Multiple studies document framework limitations in digital health:

- **COM-B/TDF** poorly capture **digital engagement contexts** (e.g., notification fatigue, passive sensing) -- PMID 40951047
- **BCTTv1** BCTs often **poorly operationalized** for app-based delivery -- PMIDs 41955893, 41880227
- **HBM** constructs (especially perceived severity and cues to action) often **fail to predict engagement** in digital environments -- PMID 41883521
- **TPB** intention-behavior gap **widens in digital contexts** where automated/algorithmic delivery disrupts volitional pathways
- **JITAIs** lack strong behavioral theory integration; decision rules often **data-driven rather than theory-driven** -- PMIDs 41943732, 41447266

### 3. Cultural Adaptation

Cultural adaptation of behavioral frameworks is an active area:
- **INSPIRE** (PMID 42438764): GenAI-assisted cultural tailoring for Black, Latinx, and Chinese populations
- Saudi Arabian **mHealth adaptation of BCW** (PMID 42204502): Cultural norms required framework modification
- Vietnam **digital adaptation fidelity** (PMID 42387528): Tensions between fidelity and cultural fit
- **General finding**: Frameworks developed in Western contexts require significant modification for non-Western populations

### 4. Digital-Specific Extensions

Emerging proposals to extend traditional frameworks:
- **Extended COM-B**: Adding technology-specific capability, opportunity, and motivation factors (PMID 41083662)
- **JITAI-specific frameworks**: Context-aware personalization frameworks (ENGAGE framework, PMID 41447266)
- **TAM-TPB integration**: Combined technology acceptance and behavioral theory models (PMID 42194436)

### 5. Framework Comparisons

- TPB and SCT remain the most commonly cited standalone theories
- COM-B/TDF is now the most common **combined framework** for intervention design
- No consensus on which framework is **most effective** for digital health
- Most comparisons are descriptive rather than predictive

## Selected Papers (24 total)

### BCTTv1 & Behavior Change Techniques

1. **Behavior Change Techniques in Digital Health Interventions for Promoting Adolescent Health Behaviors: Systematic Umbrella Review** (2026). *PMID: 42099076*. Umbrella review synthesizing BCT evidence across digital adolescent health interventions.
2. **Diet and multiple sclerosis: Behaviour change techniques in the digital health application levidex** (2026). *PMID: 41955893*. Found 28 BCTs but many poorly operationalized for digital context. **Negative finding.**
3. **Clinical Usability of Exercise Prescription Apps for Professional Use: Systematic Review** (2026). *PMID: 41880227*. Limited theory-informed BCTs in commercial apps. **Negative finding.**

### COM-B / BCW

4. **Progressing implementation of behavior change frameworks for digital health interventions: challenges and ways forward** (2026). *PMID: 41386678*. Identifies operationalization challenges for BCW in digital. **Negative finding.**
5. **Applying COM-B to Understand Adherence to Tele-Exercise Prehabilitation among Older Lung Cancer Patients** (2026). *PMID: 42124921*. COM-B qualitative study; digital divide key barrier.
6. **Facilitators and Barriers to Digital Self-Management in Older Adults With Depression: COM-B and TDF Study** (2026). *PMID: 41962010*. Identified barriers across all COM-B domains.
7. **Using BCW to develop an integrated intervention for diabetes and hypertension co-management in China** (2025). *PMID: 41339944*. Successful BCW application. **Positive finding.**
8. **Critical role of digitalization within the drug intervention ecosystem: BCW systematic review** (2026). *PMID: 41891930*. Found neglect of opportunity factors. **Negative finding.**
9. **Extended COM-B model for digital health: incorporating technology affordances** (2025). *PMID: 41083662*. Proposes digital-specific COM-B extension. **Positive finding (extension).**

### TDF (Theoretical Domains Framework)

10. **Barriers and enablers to integrating PA in breast cancer care: TDF and COM-B study** (2026). *PMID: 41770281*. 12 of 14 TDF domains relevant.
11. **Barriers and enablers to diabetes technologies use in type 1 diabetes: TDF scoping review** (2026). *PMID: 41945919*. Knowledge, skills, and environmental context key.
12. **Understanding Behavioral Influences on Eating Disorders and App Engagement Using COM-B and TDF** (2026). *PMID: 41605507*. Sex and culture moderated every domain. **Key negative finding.**

### TPB (Theory of Planned Behavior)

13. **Research trends in digital sexual media literacy interventions for adolescents: TPB scoping review** (2026). *PMID: 42419902*. TPB most common framework in this domain.
14. **Factors associated with intention to adopt digital health technologies for PA among people with disabilities: TAM-TPB** (2026). *PMID: 42194436*. Attitude and PBC predicted intention.
15. **Digital Health Intervention for Congenital Heart Disease: Theory-Driven Co-Design using TPB and BCW** (2026). *PMID: 42335295*. Successful integration. **Positive finding.**

### SCT (Social Cognitive Theory)

16. **SCT and TPB-based serial mediation model of social media and eating behaviors** (2026). *PMID: 42365042*. Social media influence mediated through SCT and TPB pathways.

### HBM (Health Belief Model)

17. **Intention to continue using corporate wellness mHealth apps in Thailand: HBM approach** (2025). *PMID: 41883521*. Cues to action not significant. **Negative finding.**
18. **HBM and mHealth for chronic disease self-management: systematic review** (2025). *PMID: 41666365*. Perceived severity fails to predict digital engagement. **Negative finding.**

### JITAI Frameworks

19. **Just-In-Time Adaptive Interventions for Weight Management: Scoping Review** (2026). *PMID: 41943732*. Limited behavioral theory in algorithms. **Negative finding.**
20. **Co-designing a JITAI for parental support of child physical activity** (2026). *PMID: 41447266*. Challenges translating constructs into decision rules. **Negative finding.**
21. **Multimodal JITAI for Occupational Stress Management: Pilot Study** (2026). *PMID: 42150167*. Feasibility shown but limited theorizing.
22. **Evaluating and Optimizing JITAIs in Wysa for Chronic Pain** (2025). *PMID: 40961490*. Protocol for optimization study.

### Cultural Adaptation

23. **INSPIRE: Cultural and linguistic tailoring for digital health intervention for chronic pain** (2026). *PMID: 42438764*. GenAI-assisted cultural adaptation framework. **Positive finding.**
24. **Co-designing culturally appropriate mHealth PA intervention for midlife women in Saudi Arabia** (2026). *PMID: 42204502*. BCW adaptation for cultural context.

### Framework Failure / Limitations

25. **Factors influencing engagement in digital substance use prevention: COM-B and TDF gaps** (2025). *PMID: 40951047*. Frameworks do not fully capture digital engagement contexts. **Negative finding.**

## Implications for PEARL

- **No single framework is sufficient** for digital health JITAIs. COM-B provides a useful starting point but needs digital-specific extension.
- **Behavioral constructs must be operationalized** for algorithm-based delivery. Many BCTs fail when translated to apps.
- **Cultural context is critical**: one-size-fits-all framework application is inadequate.
- **JITAI-specific frameworks** (like ENGAGE) are emerging but underdeveloped; PEARL's config-driven RL approach could contribute here.
- **Opportunity factors** (environmental, social) are neglected in most BCW/COM-B digital applications.
- **Theory-algorithm gap**: The weakest link is translating behavioral constructs into decision rules for RL/adaptive algorithms.
